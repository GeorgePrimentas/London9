My programming/coding experience & skills

Back in the early eighties, my older brother got a ZX-Spectrum and that was my first experience with computers. I remember that apart from playing computer games (which perhaps was the reason most people were getting a home computer back then) I showed a desire to learn to write programs. I did so using the mother ‘tongue’ of ZX-Spectrum which was a version of BASIC.

Later on, we got a Sinclair QL computer and I kept on practising/programming using an advanced version of BASIC.

In the nineties I got my first Mac (LC475) and I used it mostly for graphic design; initially as a student and then as a freelancer. It must have been around 1999/2000 when I designed my first website (using exclusively HTML). It was about an avenue in my hometown (Athens) and the neoclassical buildings someone can see on the avenue. Unfortunately, apart from the ‘splash’ page the whole content is/was in Greek only.

A few years after completing my BA in Graphic Design, I did an MA in Interactive Media. There, I employed my programming skills to develop a game for blind/visually impaired people (using sound as the main element of interaction). I used an application called Director (developed by Macromedia - bought later by Adobe) and its scripting language, Lingo (now both obsolete).

Infographics

The last 12 years I have focused on designing infographics. They are static infographics designed in Adobe Illustrator. I aim to learn D3 (JavaScript library) at some point so that the infographics I design become more dynamic / interactive and with some motion.

Other interests

Apart from graphic design and programming other interests of mine include running - I have run more than 250 parkruns (5km runs), music (from listening to the Beatles to playing several musical instruments) and visiting museums and galleries.
